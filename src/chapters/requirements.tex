\section{Feasibility Study}


\begin{table}[H]
    \centering
    \small
    \caption{Feasibility Study Summary}
    \label{tab:feasibility_study_summary}
    \renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.2}
    \begin{tabularx}{0.94\linewidth}{|X|X|}
        \hline
        \textbf{Feasibility Type} & \textbf{Summary} \\
        \hline
        \textbf{Technical} & All required data sources (OpenAQ, Open-Meteo), libraries (pandas, scikit-learn, PyTorch, PyTorch Geometric), and modeling techniques (persistence, regularized regression, GAT-GRU) are freely available and have already been used successfully in the baseline stage of this project. \\
        \hline
        \textbf{Operational} & The system operates as an offline-trained, dashboard-served research prototype; no physical sensor deployment is required, since it uses publicly available historical data. \\
        \hline
        \textbf{Economic} & Nearly all tools and datasets are free/open-source. Estimated total cost is low (see Appendix B), covering internet access, optional cloud GPU usage, hosting, and printing. \\
        \hline
        \textbf{Schedule} & The project is being developed in phases -- data pipeline, baseline models, wind-aware graph construction, GAT-GRU model, explainability, dashboard -- allowing partial progress to be demonstrated at each milestone (see Appendix A). \\
        \hline
    \end{tabularx}
\end{table}
